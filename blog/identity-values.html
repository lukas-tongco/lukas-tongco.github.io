  1. A situation where I made an ethical decision was when I noticed a kid fell into the deep end of a pool and I went to go help her. At the time I wasn’t “weighing up” any values involved in that decision, I just knew it was dangerous for the kid and that I was capable of reaching her. Reflecting on it now, I’m glad that I instinctively know what to do and that I didn’t hesitate in doing it.

  2. My Filipino culture and the people around me have influenced my values and identity by having a strong emphasis on family, community, and respect. This includes supporting and being loyal to my family and friends, being polite and respectful both in personal and professional settings, and working together with others to achieve a common goal.

  3. My strengths include an eagerness to learn, persistence and adaptability, discipline, and creative problem solving skills. These will help my learning experience as I won’t easily give up and am able to find other solutions if I get stuck.

  4. Some limitations I have in terms of my learning and career development are perfectionism, time management, and procrastination. They’ve affected me by causing delays in submitting work, but I’ve managed to improve in those areas using tools and techniques I’ve acquired, such as timeboxing and Pomodoro.

  5. An example of when I was trying to work productively with others, but there were problems, was when I was working on a coding project with two others at a previous school. One member was struggling in terms of technical skill, which hindered our progress. Along with our tutor, I would help her learn what she needed for the project. Meanwhile the other member wouldn’t respond to my messages. I tried using different platforms and telling my tutor about it but we never got a response. In the end I found out that he went overseas for vacation in the middle of the project and dropped the class. Reflecting on it now, I’m not sure what other strategies I could’ve used, as the two members were out of my control.
