Neuroplasticity

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people because it improves our brain’s ability to learn and adapt. It also helps our brain heal better from things like a stroke or head injury.

For my own benefit, I will engage with the principles of neuroplasticity by practicing known and new coding skills and engaging in meditation every day.

Some ways to increase your neuroplasticity are getting good sleep, continuously learning new things, and trying to get out of your comfort zone.

A resource that I found particularly useful is this article by Cleveland Clinic.

Growth Mindset

The growth mindset is the belief that one can improve in any area despite challenges and setbacks as long as they keep practicing. It’s relevant because it helps us become more resilient and not give up when we fail and also lets us learn better.

In this exploration, what surprised me was how much we can limit ourselves just from mindset alone. What changed for me was knowing that it’s okay to fail as it helps us get better if we’re consistent.

I will integrate growth mindset into my learning journey by reminding myself that it’s okay to not understand things at first and that as long as I keep trying I’ll be able to achieve my goals eventually.

A resource that I found particularly useful is this article from Psychology Today.

Learning Plan

These understandings have shaped my learning plan and strategy by encouraging me to try different methods of learning until I find the right one for me depending on the goal I’m trying to achieve. By understanding that my brain is “malleable” and that I don’t have a fixed intelligence motivates me to keep trying and be persistent in learning.
